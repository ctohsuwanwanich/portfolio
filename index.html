🏆 UCLA Dean's Prize for Excellence in Research & Creativity 2024

Project 01

Leaving No One Behind? Shifting Vulnerabilities of Participants in Baan Mankong Program in Bangkok, Thailand

Awarded UCLA Dean's Prize for Excellence in Research and Creativity 2024. This study examines the Baan Mankong slum redevelopment program in Bangkok, which seeks to improve housing and quality of life through community savings, collective ownership, and stakeholder collaboration. Using ethnographic fieldwork, interviews, and census data, it finds that while housing and living conditions improved, the program's rigid design increased vulnerabilities for some residents. The study calls for more adaptable policies that address pre-existing inequalities and allow greater customization, which could improve satisfaction, reduce financial strain, and support more equitable outcomes.

🏆 1st Place — UCLA Library Prize for Undergraduate Research in Humanities, Arts & Social Sciences 2025

Project 02

Gatekeeping Green Space: Minimum Lot Size Requirements, Private Lands, and Public Park Provision in Beverly Hills, Los Angeles County

Awarded the first place in UCLA Library Prize for Undergraduate Research in Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences 2025. This paper examines how affluent neighborhoods control space in Beverly Hills through zoning and park regulation. Using spatial analysis and ethnographic observation, it finds that policies like minimum lot sizes restrict public space and reinforce inequality. Parks in northern areas function as semi-private extensions of luxury homes, while southern parks, though more community-oriented, still face regulatory barriers. Analysis of planning decisions shows how affluent residents shape policies that prioritize property values over accessibility. The study calls for planning reforms to create more inclusive public spaces that better serve diverse communities.
